# Story

## Purpose of This Document

This document is the narrative and visual brief for the game's two storylines.
It describes what happens in each chapter, what tone each arc should carry, what
each illustration should depict, and what particle effects should accompany each
cutscene. The actual story text lives in `stories.ts`; this document is the
source of truth for the intent behind it and must stay in sync with it.

---

## Story Synopses

### Pokemon — Adventure - Rescue

Rowlet goes missing. Pikachu finds a pink feather. What follows is a rescue
mission across five environments — (the kidnapper is Fluttershy, an
animal-loving pony from another world, spotted Rowlet and was so enchanted she
took him home to care for him, not realizing he belonged somewhere else, but we
don't need to show this we can lightly imply it). Pikachu fights through twenty
levels of keyboard corruption to reach Rowlet. The climax is a full Thunderbolt
that shatters the dimensional barrier and brings Rowlet home. Fluttershy sees
Pikachu's face, understands what she did, and apologizes. The story is earnest
and warm, with light comedy from Pikachu's untranslatable determination. The
emotional payoff is the zap.

### MLP — Friendship Lesson

Pinkie Pie is at a party when she notices water sprayed everywhere. Squirtle's
pranks aren't cruel in intent, but they're disruptive — water blasts at wrong
moments, things going missing, chaos at the worst times. Including all over
Pinkie Pie's party. Pinkie, who loves a good prank but knows the difference
between fun and unkind, decides to teach Squirtle what real friendship looks
like. The story is Pinkie at her best: absurd, warm, genuinely wise. She has to
clean keys with rainbows and friendship. By the finale, Squirtle helps set up
the party himself — no pranks, just showing up. That's when he gets it.

## Pokemon Cutscenes

### Cutscene 0

Plays before any gameplay. Sets up the premise.

#### Text

Rowlet was delivering a very important message when it happened. One moment he
was gliding through the Digital Grove, the next — a pair of wide, gentle eyes
appeared from nowhere. A soft yellow hoof reached out. "Oh," breathed a voice.
"Oh, you are the most precious little bird I have ever seen."

Rowlet did not have time to hoot before he was scooped up and carried through a
shimmering gap between worlds.

Pikachu returned to find the message bag on the ground and a single pink feather
drifting down to meet it. His cheeks sparked once. Then he turned to the
keyboard.

#### Illustration description

The Digital Grove at dusk, soft green light filtering through pixel-trees.
Pikachu crouches near a small message bag lying on the ground, one paw raised to
catch a single pink feather mid-fall. His expression is focused and worried, not
panicked. The keyboard behind him shows faint signs of corruption — a few keys
flickering. The scene should feel quiet rather than dramatic: a discovery, not a
disaster yet.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Soft floating leaves drifting down through the grove, gentle
  and calm, establishing the peaceful Digital Grove atmosphere.
- **One-time:** A brief swirl of pink flower petals — Fluttershy's trail —
  drifting across the screen and then gone.

### Cutscene 1

After Arc 1. First progress. The trail comes into focus.

#### Text

Four keys restored. The Digital Grove felt a little lighter, a little less
corrupted. Pikachu followed the trail of flower petals deeper into the forest,
eyes low to the ground, ears up.

Then he stopped.

Through a gap in the trees, far in the distance, a warm light glowed. A small
yellow cottage, tucked between two ancient pixel-oaks. And in the window — a
round green shape, silhouetted against the light. Not moving. Just sitting.

Pikachu stared for a long moment. "Pika," he said, quietly. Which meant: _I see
you. I'm coming._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu standing in the Digital Grove with two glowing golden keys — F and J —
floating beside him, radiating warm light. The grove behind him is partially
restored: bright trees on one side, flickering corruption on the other. Far in
the background, barely visible through the trees, a small cozy yellow cottage
with warm light in the window. In the window: the unmistakable round silhouette
of Rowlet. Pikachu is looking directly at it, expression steady.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves and soft golden motes from the restored keys,
  drifting lazily upward.
- **One-time:** A small burst of golden light as the restored keys pulse — a
  brief flash of warmth and progress.

### Cutscene 2

After Arc 2. The Thunder Shrine. Pikachu sees what's happening on the other
side.

#### Text

Eight keys cleansed. The Thunder Shrine crackled around Pikachu as he pushed
through the storm.

Then the static shifted — and in the electric haze, a vision took shape.

A cottage interior, warm and cluttered with flowers. Fluttershy sat cross-legged
on the floor, humming softly, arranging petals around a small woven nest. In the
nest: Rowlet, feathers slightly puffed, watching the door.

She wasn't cruel. That was almost worse. She was being very, very kind.

Pikachu's cheeks sparked. He turned back to the keyboard. _Faster._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu at the Thunder Shrine — a stone structure crackling with golden
lightning. Above the shrine, a vision floats in the electric storm like a window
into another world: the interior of Fluttershy's cottage. Fluttershy is seen
from the back, placing flowers gently around Rowlet's nest. Rowlet sits in the
nest looking toward the door with a searching expression. The vision is soft and
warm against the harsh crackling shrine. Pikachu looks at it from below, cheeks
sparking, expression sharp.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crackling electric sparks and lightning arcs, the full storm
  atmosphere of the Thunder Shrine.
- **One-time:** A burst of static that parts like a curtain to reveal the
  cottage vision — brief, dramatic, then fading back to static.

### Cutscene 3

After Arc 3. The Crystal Cavern. Sound carries through the walls.

#### Text

In the Crystal Cavern, the walls were thin enough to carry sound from the other
side.

Pikachu heard Rowlet first — a low, searching hoot, the kind that meant _I know
something is wrong but I can't place it._ Then another voice, gentle and close:
"Shh. You're safe here, little one. I'll take good care of you. You don't need
to go anywhere."

Pikachu pressed his paw flat against the crystal. He could almost feel the
warmth on the other side.

He typed harder.

#### Illustration description

Pikachu in the Crystal Cavern, one paw pressed flat against a glowing crystal
wall. His cheeks spark faintly. The cavern around him sparkles with restored
letters — beautiful, cold, blue-lit. Through the crystal wall, distorted but
visible: Fluttershy's silhouette leaning tenderly over Rowlet's nest, one wing
outstretched. The image is quiet and tense. Closeness without contact.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Soft crystal sparkles drifting through the cavern — slow,
  cold, blue-white.
- **One-time:** A gentle ripple pulse through the crystal wall when Pikachu
  touches it, like a stone dropped in water.

### Cutscene 4

After Arc 4. Stardrift Coast. The final approach. The energy builds.

#### Text

Sixteen keys. The Stardrift Coast stretched out behind Pikachu, glittering and
wide. Ahead: the Apex Summit, and beyond it, the crossing point.

Four more keys. Then Rowlet.

Pikachu stood at the base of the summit and felt it — the charge he'd been
building across sixteen levels of restored electricity. His cheeks glowed
steadily, not flickering. Ready.

He had been saving it up. He knew exactly what it was for.

_Four more keys._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu on the Stardrift Coast at the base of the Apex Summit, facing upward.
The ocean glitters behind him. The summit looms ahead, wrapped in cold mist.
Pikachu's cheeks glow intensely — more than usual, a deep steady gold rather
than the usual flicker. His posture is coiled and completely still. In the mist
at the summit's peak, four faint letter shapes: V, X, Q, Z. His expression is
calm. He is ready.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Ocean spray and coastal wind particles — light, horizontal
  drift.
- **One-time:** A crackling pulse of electric sparks off Pikachu's cheeks —
  brief, controlled, a warning of what's coming.

---

### Cutscene 5

After Arc 5. The finale. The big zap.

#### Text

The final key glowed gold. The last wall of corruption crumbled. And there,
beyond the summit: the crossing point, open and clear. Fluttershy's cottage,
right there.

Pikachu didn't think. He just ran three steps forward, planted both feet, and
let everything go.

_THUNDERBOLT._

Golden light exploded across the sky. The dimensional barrier shattered like
glass. Rowlet burst free with a startled hoot and immediately began spiraling
toward Pikachu at full speed.

The dust settled. Fluttershy picked herself up slowly. She looked at Pikachu's
face — still glowing, still catching his breath. She looked at Rowlet, already
burrowed under Pikachu's ear, hooting softly.

"Oh," she said. A small sound. "Oh. He wasn't lost, was he. He had someone."

She looked at the ground. "I'm so sorry. I didn't ask."

Rowlet glanced at her once — not unkindly — then tucked his head back in.

The keyboard was clean. Every key, warm and gold. Pikachu sat down.

Home.

#### Illustration description

The Apex Summit split into two emotional moments — consider this a wide
illustration with two focal points. On the left: Pikachu mid-Thunderbolt, cheeks
erupting with massive golden lightning that fills the sky, feet planted, fully
committed. The dimensional barrier behind him shatters like stained glass. On
the right: the aftermath — Pikachu sitting, Rowlet tucked under his ear, both of
them still and content. At the edge of the frame, Fluttershy stands with one
hoof raised to her mouth, expression soft and sad and understanding. The
keyboard in the background glows clean gold. The image should feel explosive and
then immediately tender.

#### Particle effects

- **One-time (1):** Massive golden lightning explosion — the largest particle
  event in the game. Full-screen burst of electric gold, blinding for a moment.
- **One-time (2):** Shattering glass fragments — the dimensional barrier
  breaking apart, pieces catching the light as they fall.
- **Persistent (after):** Soft golden sparkles raining slowly down, warm and
  peaceful. The storm is over.

## MLP Cutscenes

### Cutscene 0

Plays before any gameplay. Sets up the premise.

#### Text

Pinkie Pie was typing the finest party invitations Equestria had ever seen when
the window burst open.

A small blue turtle wearing sunglasses landed directly on her keyboard. Then he
turned around, aimed, and water-blasted every invitation into soggy confetti.

He laughed. Pinkie stared at the wreckage. Then she looked at him — really
looked. The too-wide grin. The hopeful eyes. The way he was waiting to see if it
worked.

"Oh, you sweet little guy," Pinkie said. "You have _no idea_ how this works, do
you."

She grabbed a fresh stack of paper. She had a new project.

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie's party-planning desk in the Digital Grove, completely drenched.
Soggy invitations droop everywhere. On the keyboard: Squirtle in sunglasses,
looking very pleased with himself. Pinkie stands across from him, dripping
slightly, holding a fresh stack of dry paper. Her expression is not angry — it's
delighted and a little pitying, the look of someone who has just spotted a
wonderful problem to solve. Party decorations are everywhere. It is chaos.
Pinkie is in her element.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves and soft motes in the Digital Grove.
- **One-time:** A spray of water particles bursting across the screen —
  Squirtle's water blast, wide and chaotic.

---

### Cutscene 1

After Arc 1. First lesson. What makes a good prank.

#### Text

"Rule one," Pinkie said, settling cross-legged in front of Squirtle. "A good
prank makes _everyone_ laugh. Even the pony it's happening to. _Especially_
them."

Squirtle tilted his head.

Pinkie demonstrated. She had prepared very carefully. The prank was ridiculous,
perfectly timed, and completely harmless — and when it landed, the pony it
happened to laughed so hard she fell over.

Squirtle watched. Then he looked at his own hands. Then back at Pinkie.

"It's different," he said slowly. "When they laugh too."

"It's everything," Pinkie said.

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie sitting across from Squirtle in the Digital Grove, both of them
facing each other like a student and a very enthusiastic teacher. Two glowing
keys — F and J — float in the air nearby, giving the scene a warm, restored
quality. Pinkie has one hoof raised mid-explanation. Squirtle looks uncertain
but genuinely curious. The grove is bright and partially restored behind them.
The F key and J key have small faces and look like they're listening too.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves, golden motes from restored keys.
- **One-time:** A burst of pink sparkles and tiny confetti — Pinkie's prank
  landing off-screen, implied by the celebration particles.

---

### Cutscene 2

After Arc 2. Getting it wrong. The line between funny and hurtful.

#### Text

Squirtle tried. He really did.

But the timing was off, and the water blast caught Fluttershy mid-sentence
during her bird choir rehearsal. The birds scattered. Fluttershy's baton
drooped. She didn't say anything. That was worse.

Pinkie put a hoof on Squirtle's shoulder.

"Did she laugh?" she asked.

Squirtle shook his head.

"That's the line. Right there. The moment before you do it — if you can't
picture them laughing, _don't._"

Squirtle looked at the soggy choir stand. Something in his expression changed.

#### Illustration description

The Thunder Shrine, electric and dramatic. In the foreground: Squirtle looking
at the ground, sunglasses slightly askew. Pinkie crouches beside him, not
scolding — explaining, gently, with a hoof on his shoulder. In the background:
Fluttershy (a brief cameo) helping scattered birds back to their perches, her
expression patient and forgiving. Eight glowing keys orbit the scene. The
contrast between the stormy shrine atmosphere and Pinkie's warm body language is
intentional.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crackling electric sparks, the Thunder Shrine's storm.
- **One-time:** A water splash burst — Squirtle's misfired prank — chaotic and
  immediate, then gone.

---

### Cutscene 3

After Arc 3. The first good prank. Something clicks.

#### Text

It was small. Just a whoopee cushion under the right chair at the right moment.

But Pinkie had coached every detail — who, when, how — and when it went off, the
pony laughed, and Squirtle laughed, and Pinkie laughed so hard she slid off a
rock.

Squirtle went quiet for a moment after.

"They laughed _because of me_," he said. "Not at me. Because of me."

"Yep," Pinkie said.

"That's never happened before."

Pinkie just smiled and didn't say anything, which was very unusual for her.

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie and Squirtle in the Crystal Cavern, both mid-laugh — the genuine
kind, leaning toward each other. The Crystal Cavern sparkles beautifully around
them. Twelve glowing keys float in a loose cluster overhead. Squirtle's
sunglasses are slightly fogged from laughing. Pinkie's expression is pure joy
but there's something knowing in it. This is the emotional center of the MLP arc
— the moment Squirtle gets it.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crystal sparkles drifting slowly, blue-white and beautiful.
- **One-time:** A pop of confetti and a tiny cartoon "poof" burst — the prank
  landing. Small, delightful, perfectly timed.

---

### Cutscene 4

After Arc 4. Squirtle asks the real question.

#### Text

They'd pulled off four good pranks together. Squirtle had a notebook now. He was
taking this seriously.

"Pinkie," he said, watching the Stardrift Coast glitter. "Is this what
friendship is? Pranks?"

Pinkie thought about it for a whole two seconds, which was a long time for her.

"Pranks are the fun part," she said. "But the real part is simpler. You pick
someone. And then you just... show up for them. Whatever they need. You show
up."

Squirtle turned the notebook over in his hands.

"I've never done that either," he said.

"I know," Pinkie said. "You're going to be really good at it."

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie and Squirtle sitting side by side on the Stardrift Coast, looking out
at the glittering ocean. Squirtle holds a small notebook. Pinkie has her legs
dangling off the edge of a rock, expression unusually still and thoughtful. The
coast is wide and open and the light is golden. This is the quietest
illustration of the MLP arc — no pranks, no chaos, just two friends watching the
water.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Ocean spray and light coastal wind, gentle horizontal drift.
- **One-time:** A slow, soft scatter of pink sparkles — not celebratory, just
  warm. Like a moment settling.

---

### Cutscene 5

After Arc 5. The party. Squirtle gets it.

#### Text

The party was everything Pinkie had planned. Bigger, actually. Squirtle had
helped with the decorations and it turned out he had very strong opinions about
banner placement.

There were no pranks. He didn't plan any. It didn't occur to him.

Halfway through, Pinkie found him in the corner surrounded by three ponies who
were all trying to teach him a hoof-bump at the same time. He was laughing. They
were laughing. He wasn't performing anything. He was just there.

Pinkie watched from across the room. The keyboard behind her glowed clean and
whole, every key warm and content.

She smiled. _That's the one,_ she thought. _That's the whole lesson, right
there._

#### Illustration description

A grand party in full swing on the Apex Summit — the finale location transformed
into something warm and festive. Center frame: Squirtle surrounded by three
ponies in a chaotic, joyful hoof-bump lesson. His sunglasses are pushed up on
his head. He's genuinely laughing. Across the room, Pinkie watches with a soft,
satisfied expression — not the center of attention for once, just happy to see
it. All 26 keys glow warmly in the background like decorations. Streamers,
balloons, confetti everywhere. The party cannon is visible but not fired — it's
done its job.

#### Particle effects

- **One-time (1):** Party cannon blast — a massive burst of confetti, streamers,
  and color filling the screen. The biggest celebratory particle event in the
  game.
- **Persistent (after):** Confetti drifting slowly down, warm and festive, for
  the rest of the cutscene.
