# Story

## Purpose of This Document

This document is the narrative and visual brief for the game's two storylines.
It describes what happens in each chapter, what tone each arc should carry, what
each illustration should depict, and what particle effects should accompany each
cutscene. The actual story text lives in `stories.ts`; this document is the
source of truth for the intent behind it and must stay in sync with it.

---

## Story Synopses

### Pokemon - Adventure Rescue

Rowlet vanishes mid-delivery. Pikachu finds the dropped message bag and a
single pink feather, then follows the trail across five environments of growing
keyboard corruption. The truth is gentle rather than villainous: Fluttershy, an
animal-loving pony from another world, found Rowlet irresistible and took him
home to care for him without realizing he already belonged to someone. That
intent should stay mostly implied until the later cutscenes. Pikachu's side of
the story is simple and forceful: restore the keyboard, close the distance, and
bring Rowlet home. The climax is a full Thunderbolt that shatters the barrier
between worlds. Fluttershy understands her mistake immediately and apologizes.
The arc should feel earnest, warm, and a little funny because Pikachu is so
completely, undeniably serious about rescuing his tiny owl friend.

### MLP - Friendship Lesson

Pinkie Pie is preparing a party when Squirtle turns it into a soaked, slapstick
disaster. His pranks are not malicious, but they are careless: water blasts at
the wrong moment, props go missing, and the joke keeps landing on everyone
else. Pinkie loves chaos when everyone is in on it, so she recognizes the real
problem immediately. Instead of punishing him, she decides to teach him the
difference between attention and friendship. The arc should showcase Pinkie at
her best: absurd, kind, and surprisingly wise. Each cutscene moves Squirtle
from "I did something" toward "I showed up for someone." By the finale, he is
helping with the party without even thinking about a prank first. That is the
moment he finally gets it.

## Pokemon Cutscenes

### Cutscene 0

Plays before any gameplay. Sets up the premise.

#### Text

Rowlet was halfway through his delivery route when something reached through
the trees and lifted him out of the grove. Pikachu found the message bag on the
ground, a few scattered letters beside it, and a single pink feather drifting
down to meet them. His cheeks sparked once.

#### Illustration description

The Digital Grove at dusk, soft green light filtering through pixel-trees.
Pikachu crouches near the dropped message bag, one paw raised to catch a single
pink feather mid-fall. Beside the bag are a few loose delivery letters and one
small Rowlet feather, making it clear who was just here. Far in the background,
barely visible between the trees, Rowlet's round silhouette is being carried
away into a swirl of pink petals. Pikachu's expression is focused and worried,
not panicked. The keyboard behind him shows faint signs of corruption - a few
keys flickering. The scene should feel quiet rather than explosive: a discovery
made one second too late.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Soft floating leaves drifting down through the grove, gentle
  and calm, establishing the peaceful Digital Grove atmosphere.
- **One-time:** A brief swirl of pink flower petals - Fluttershy's trail -
  drifting across the screen and then gone.

---

### Cutscene 1

After Arc 1. First progress. The trail comes into focus.

#### Text

Four keys restored. The petal trail led deeper into the grove and then, through
the trees, to a warm glow: a yellow cottage. Rowlet's silhouette sat in the
window. "Pika." _I see you. I'm coming._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu standing in the Digital Grove with two glowing golden keys - F and J -
floating beside him, radiating warm light. The grove behind him is partially
restored: bright trees on one side, flickering corruption on the other. Far in
the background, barely visible through the trees, a small cozy yellow cottage
with warm light in the window. In the window: the unmistakable round silhouette
of Rowlet. Pikachu is looking directly at it, expression steady.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves and soft golden motes from the restored keys,
  drifting lazily upward.
- **One-time:** A small burst of golden light as the restored keys pulse - a
  brief flash of warmth and progress.

---

### Cutscene 2

After Arc 2. The Thunder Shrine. Pikachu sees what's happening on the other
side.

#### Text

Through the electric haze came a vision: a warm cottage, flower petals, Rowlet
in a little nest, watching the door. She was being very, very kind. That almost
made it worse. Pikachu's cheeks sparked. _Faster._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu at the Thunder Shrine - a stone structure crackling with golden
lightning. Above the shrine, a vision floats in the electric storm like a
window into another world: the interior of Fluttershy's cottage. Fluttershy is
seen from the back, placing flowers gently around Rowlet's nest. Rowlet sits in
the nest looking toward the door with a searching expression. The vision is
soft and warm against the harsh crackling shrine. Pikachu looks at it from
below, cheeks sparking, expression sharp.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crackling electric sparks and lightning arcs, the full storm
  atmosphere of the Thunder Shrine.
- **One-time:** A burst of static that parts like a curtain to reveal the
  cottage vision - brief, dramatic, then fading back to static.

---

### Cutscene 3

After Arc 3. The Crystal Cavern. Sound carries through the walls.

#### Text

Through the crystal came a low, searching hoot. Then a soft voice: "You don't
need to go anywhere." Pikachu pressed his paw flat against the wall and typed
harder.

#### Illustration description

Pikachu in the Crystal Cavern, one paw pressed flat against a glowing crystal
wall. His cheeks spark faintly. The cavern around him sparkles with restored
letters - beautiful, cold, blue-lit. Through the crystal wall, distorted but
visible: Fluttershy's silhouette leaning tenderly over Rowlet's nest, one wing
outstretched. The image is quiet and tense. Closeness without contact.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Soft crystal sparkles drifting through the cavern - slow,
  cold, blue-white.
- **One-time:** A gentle ripple pulse through the crystal wall when Pikachu
  touches it, like a stone dropped in water.

---

### Cutscene 4

After Arc 4. Stardrift Coast. The final approach. The energy builds.

#### Text

Sixteen keys. The summit ahead. Pikachu's cheeks glowed steady, not flickering.
He had been saving that charge for one thing only. _Four more keys._

#### Illustration description

Pikachu on the Stardrift Coast at the base of the Apex Summit, facing upward.
The ocean glitters behind him. The summit looms ahead, wrapped in cold mist.
Pikachu's cheeks glow intensely - more than usual, a deep steady gold rather
than the usual flicker. His posture is coiled and completely still. In the mist
at the summit's peak, four faint letter shapes: V, X, Q, Z. His expression is
calm. He is ready.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Ocean spray and coastal wind particles - light, horizontal
  drift.
- **One-time:** A crackling pulse of electric sparks off Pikachu's cheeks -
  brief, controlled, a warning of what's coming.

---

### Cutscene 5

After Arc 5. The finale. The big zap.

#### Text

The last key. Pikachu planted his feet and let everything go. _THUNDERBOLT._
The barrier shattered. Rowlet came spiraling home. A soft voice in the dust:
"Oh. He had someone. I'm so sorry." Rowlet glanced back once, then tucked in.

#### Illustration description

The Apex Summit split into two emotional moments - consider this a wide
illustration with two focal points. On the left: Pikachu mid-Thunderbolt,
cheeks erupting with massive golden lightning that fills the sky, feet planted,
fully committed. The dimensional barrier behind him shatters like stained
glass. On the right: the aftermath - Pikachu sitting, Rowlet tucked under his
ear, both of them still and content. At the edge of the frame, Fluttershy
stands with one hoof raised to her mouth, expression soft and sad and
understanding. The keyboard in the background glows clean gold. The image
should feel explosive and then immediately tender.

#### Particle effects

- **One-time (1):** Massive golden lightning explosion - the largest particle
  event in the game. Full-screen burst of electric gold, blinding for a moment.
- **One-time (2):** Shattering glass fragments - the dimensional barrier
  breaking apart, pieces catching the light as they fall.
- **Persistent (after):** Soft golden sparkles raining slowly down, warm and
  peaceful. The storm is over.

## MLP Cutscenes

### Cutscene 0

Plays before any gameplay. Sets up the premise.

#### Text

Pinkie's invitations were perfect until something small, blue, and very wet
crashed through the window and blasted them into soggy confetti. She looked at
the little turtle. He grinned back. "Oh, honey," she said. "We need to talk."

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie's party-planning desk in the Digital Grove, completely drenched.
Soggy invitations droop everywhere. On the keyboard: Squirtle in sunglasses,
looking very pleased with himself. Pinkie stands across from him, dripping
slightly, holding a fresh stack of dry paper. Her expression is not angry - it
is delighted and a little pitying, the look of someone who has just spotted a
wonderful problem to solve. Party decorations are everywhere. It is chaos.
Pinkie is in her element.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves and soft motes in the Digital Grove.
- **One-time:** A spray of water particles bursting across the screen -
  Squirtle's water blast, wide and chaotic.

---

### Cutscene 1

After Arc 1. First lesson. What makes a good prank.

#### Text

"Rule one," Pinkie said. "Everyone laughs, _especially_ the pony it happened
to." She demonstrated. It worked. The little turtle stared at his hands. "It's
different when they laugh too." "It's everything," said Pinkie.

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie sitting across from Squirtle in the Digital Grove, both of them
facing each other like a student and a very enthusiastic teacher. Two glowing
keys - F and J - float in the air nearby, giving the scene a warm, restored
quality. Pinkie has one hoof raised mid-explanation. Squirtle looks uncertain
but genuinely curious. The grove is bright and partially restored behind them.
The F key and J key have small faces and look like they're listening too.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Floating leaves, golden motes from restored keys.
- **One-time:** A burst of pink sparkles and tiny confetti - Pinkie's prank
  landing off-screen, implied by the celebration particles.

---

### Cutscene 2

After Arc 2. Getting it wrong. The line between funny and hurtful.

#### Text

He tried. The timing was off. A bird choir scattered and nobody laughed. Pinkie
put a hoof on his shoulder. "If you can't picture them smiling, don't."
Something in his expression changed.

#### Illustration description

The Thunder Shrine, electric and dramatic. In the foreground: Squirtle looking
at the ground, sunglasses slightly askew. Pinkie crouches beside him, not
scolding - explaining, gently, with a hoof on his shoulder. In the background:
Fluttershy (a brief cameo) helping scattered birds back to their perches, her
expression patient and forgiving. Eight glowing keys orbit the scene. The
contrast between the stormy shrine atmosphere and Pinkie's warm body language
is intentional.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crackling electric sparks, the Thunder Shrine's storm.
- **One-time:** A water splash burst - Squirtle's misfired prank - chaotic and
  immediate, then gone.

---

### Cutscene 3

After Arc 3. The first good prank. Something clicks.

#### Text

A whoopee cushion. Perfect timing. The pony laughed, and so did the little
turtle. "They laughed _because of me_," he said quietly. "That's never happened
before." Pinkie smiled and, unusually, said nothing.

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie and Squirtle in the Crystal Cavern, both mid-laugh - the genuine
kind, leaning toward each other. The Crystal Cavern sparkles beautifully around
them. Twelve glowing keys float in a loose cluster overhead. Squirtle's
sunglasses are slightly fogged from laughing. Pinkie's expression is pure joy,
but there's something knowing in it. This is the emotional center of the MLP
arc - the moment Squirtle gets it.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Crystal sparkles drifting slowly, blue-white and beautiful.
- **One-time:** A pop of confetti and a tiny cartoon "poof" burst - the prank
  landing. Small, delightful, perfectly timed.

---

### Cutscene 4

After Arc 4. Squirtle asks the real question.

#### Text

"Is this what friendship is? Pranks?" Pinkie thought for two whole seconds.
"Pranks are the fun part. The real part? You pick someone and you show up." He
turned his notebook over. "I've never done that." "I know," she said. "You're
going to be great at it."

#### Illustration description

Pinkie Pie and Squirtle sitting side by side on the Stardrift Coast, looking
out at the glittering ocean. Squirtle holds a small notebook. Pinkie has her
legs dangling off the edge of a rock, expression unusually still and
thoughtful. The coast is wide and open and the light is golden. This is the
quietest illustration of the MLP arc - no pranks, no chaos, just two friends
watching the water.

#### Particle effects

- **Persistent:** Ocean spray and light coastal wind, gentle horizontal drift.
- **One-time:** A slow, soft scatter of pink sparkles - not celebratory, just
  warm. Like a moment settling.

---

### Cutscene 5

After Arc 5. The party. Squirtle gets it.

#### Text

The party happened. He helped with the decorations - no pranks, and they never
even crossed his mind. Halfway through, Pinkie spotted him surrounded by new
friends, just laughing. _That's the one,_ she thought. _That's the whole
lesson._

#### Illustration description

A grand party in full swing on the Apex Summit - the finale location
transformed into something warm and festive. Center frame: Squirtle surrounded
by three ponies in a chaotic, joyful hoof-bump lesson. His sunglasses are
pushed up on his head. He's genuinely laughing. Across the room, Pinkie watches
with a soft, satisfied expression - not the center of attention for once, just
happy to see it. All 26 keys glow warmly in the background like decorations.
Streamers, balloons, confetti everywhere. The party cannon is visible but not
fired - it's done its job.

#### Particle effects

- **One-time (1):** Party cannon blast - a massive burst of confetti,
  streamers, and color filling the screen. The biggest celebratory particle
  event in the game.
- **Persistent (after):** Confetti drifting slowly down, warm and festive, for
  the rest of the cutscene.
